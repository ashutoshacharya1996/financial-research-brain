## What Changed in the Research Queue

The same 6 stocks, but reranked and with new information on each.

---

**1. BEL (Bharat Electronics)** *(was #1, still #1)*

*What's new:* The QRSAM missile radar contract is now confirmed at ₹30,000 crore. That's not speculation — it comes from official defence reporting and BEL's own July 2026 timeline. BEL gets approximately 70% of the contract (~₹21,000 crore). The other 30% goes to BDL (Bharat Dynamics).

*Why this matters:* BEL's current order book is ₹73,882 crore. If QRSAM signs in July, the order book goes to ~₹94,000 crore on the same day — a 40% increase from one signature. That's the kind of binary event that changes a stock's story overnight. Delivery starts roughly 18 months later (around January 2028), so this is about the order book now, not the profits next quarter.

*The risk:* QRSAM was originally supposed to sign in February or March 2026. It slipped to July. Indian defence contracts slip — this is normal. But it's worth knowing that "July 2026" is already the second target date.

---

**2. HAL (Hindustan Aeronautics)** *(was #2, still #2)*

*What's new:* HAL's full-year profit (FY26) came in at ₹9,115 crore — which works out to a net profit margin of 27.6%. To put that in perspective: most manufacturers operate on 5–12% margins. HAL making 27.6% is genuinely unusual and suggests the defence pricing model gives them enormous pricing power.

Also new: India's ₹30,000 crore drone programme (MALE UAV) just closed its bidding round with 10 companies competing. HAL is one of them. The contract gives 2 winners (not 1) production lines worth roughly ₹15,000 crore each. This is good for HAL and LT both potentially winning.

*The risk:* HAL's LCA fighter jet deliveries were delayed before (from FY26 to August/September 2026). We're still waiting to see if August holds. If it slips again, HAL's full-year revenue guidance is at risk.

---

**3. JNKINDIA (JNK India)** *(was #3, still #3)*

*What's new:* The UAE order from ADNOC isn't just a press release anymore. JNK India filed it with the stock exchanges (NSE and BSE) on June 8, 2026. The filing names the client (CC7 Emirates Engineering Solutions), the project (TA'ZIZ Salt Project, ADNOC, Abu Dhabi), and the value (₹100–300 crore). The scope is full engineering and commissioning — not just selling equipment.

*Why this matters:* When a company files something with the stock exchange, it's a regulated disclosure. Press releases can be vague or optimistic. Exchange filings are on the record. This moved JNK India's first international order from "promising announcement" to "confirmed fact."

*What hasn't changed:* This is still JNK India's first export order. 97% of their business is still domestic. One ADNOC order is a great start — it's not a transformed business yet.

---

**4. PARAS (Paras Defence & Space Technologies)** *(was #5, moved up to #4)*

*What's new:* For the first time, Paras publicly disclosed its total order book: ₹928 crore. That's 1.9 times their annual revenue — meaning they have almost two years of backlog to work through. This matters because Paras doesn't hold earnings calls (unlike the larger companies), so getting any forward-visibility numbers out of them is unusual.

The BEL subcontract (₹52.82 crore, electro-optics equipment) is also now confirmed as a regulatory filing — not just a press announcement.

*Why this moved up:* The order book disclosure gives Paras revenue visibility for the first time. Combined with their PAT growing 89% when revenue grew 58%, the operating leverage story is now supported by concrete numbers.

---

**5. GABRIEL (Gabriel India)** *(was #6, moved up to #5)*

*What's new:* Gabriel's full-year numbers are now confirmed. Revenue: ₹4,667 crore (+15%). Profit after tax: ₹252.2 crore (+14.8%). And Motilal Oswal's Buy rating (₹1,266 target) is now grounded in those actual numbers — not just projections. The analyst projects profit growing 55% per year through FY28 based on Gabriel rolling out new products (sunroofs, electric vehicle parts) while keeping their auto shock absorber base.

*The one number that matters:* Motilal Oswal projects Gabriel's return on capital employed (ROCE — basically how much profit the company generates from every rupee it invests) rising from ~30% today to 37.7% by FY28. If you're comparing companies to put in a portfolio, ROCE is one of the cleanest signals of business quality. 37.7% is high.

*Still a risk:* The 55% profit CAGR is a forecast, not a guarantee. Gabriel needs to execute on the new products.

---

**6. LT (Larsen & Toubro)** *(was #4, dropped to #6)*

*What's new — and it's negative:* LT's Q4 FY26 profit came in at ₹5,326 crore — which is *3% lower* than the same quarter a year ago. Saturday's brief had the number wrong (it said +5%, based on preliminary data). The confirmed number is -3%. Also, LT's EBITDA margin for the quarter was 10.4% — down from prior levels.

*Why this matters more than it might seem:* LT has the biggest order book in India's private sector (₹7.4 trillion). Having a huge order book and shrinking profits at the same time is the exact opposite of what the India capex cycle thesis is supposed to look like. It means LT is winning lots of contracts but either pricing them too thin or struggling to execute them profitably.

*What's still positive:* LT did submit a bid for the ₹30,000 crore drone programme. Their long-term targets (₹5.8 trillion revenue by FY31) are ambitious. And the K9 Vajra artillery contract (₹7,628.70 crore — now confirmed to the exact rupee) shows defence is a real revenue line. But profit quality needs to recover before this deserves high conviction.

*The question to hold:* Is LT's Q4 margin problem a one-quarter blip (project timing, Middle East delays) or is it structural? Q1 FY27 results in July will answer this. Don't upgrade conviction until that data is in.

## The 2 Big Ideas — Updated Scores

---

**Idea #1: India's Defence Electronics Supply Chain** *(Confidence upgraded: 7.5 → 8.3)*

*What changed:*
The data quality improved significantly this week. We now have confirmed numbers (not estimates) for three major items: QRSAM at exactly ₹30,000 crore, BEL's share at exactly ~₹21,000 crore, and the MALE UAV tender now formally closed with 10 named bidders. When the same thesis gets confirmed by specific rupee values across multiple programmes, confidence goes up.

*The short version of the thesis:*
India is spending heavily on its own defence industry. Four companies — BEL, HAL, LT, and Paras — are all confirmed participants in contracts worth a combined ~₹1 trillion over the next 4 years. The near-term test is July 2026: does QRSAM sign or slip again?

*What could go wrong (required disclosure at 8.3/10):*
1. QRSAM already slipped once (February/March → July). Procurement delays in India are common. Another slip pushes BEL's order book event further out.
2. HAL's LCA jet deliveries have slipped before. If August/September slips again, FY27 revenue is at risk.
3. The drone programme has 10 bidders. Any given company has roughly a 20% chance of winning anything.
4. LT's weak margin shows that even winning massive defence contracts doesn't guarantee profit.

*Your next move:* Watch for official QRSAM signing news in July. If it closes, BEL's order book grows 40% in one day and the thesis is validated by execution, not just expectation.

---

**Idea #2: India's Industrial Capex Cycle** *(Confidence downgraded: 7.8 → 7.5 — still a high-conviction idea, but with a real warning sign)*

*What changed:*
Two things moved the score in opposite directions. The positive: JNK India's UAE order is now a confirmed exchange filing (not a press report), and Gabriel's quality as a business was confirmed by actual profit numbers. The negative — and it's a real one — is LT's Q4 profit number being 3% lower than last year. LT is the biggest company in this group by a large margin (₹82,762 crore in Q4 revenue). When the biggest company in your thesis shows shrinking profits alongside growing order books, it's a warning sign that the thesis is more complicated than it looks.

*The one-sentence summary:*
India is placing huge orders across industries — but the biggest beneficiary (LT) isn't currently converting those orders into profits, which is exactly what the thesis requires.

*Still worth tracking because:* Five other companies are all showing the opposite pattern (growing revenue, healthy margins). One company being the exception is a caution flag, not a thesis breaker — but it's worth watching.

*Your next move:* Wait for LT Q1 FY27 results (July 2026). If margins recover, downgrade the concern. If margins compress further, reduce conviction on the entire capex cycle theme.

## Jargon Glossary

| Term used | What it actually means |
|---|---|
| Order book | The total value of contracts a company has signed but not yet completed and billed |
| Order inflows | New contracts signed this quarter (the "fresh orders" number) |
| Corroboration count | How many completely separate companies mentioned the same theme independently |
| Confidence score (8.3/10) | How sure the engine is that this signal is real, based on number of sources and quality of evidence |
| EBITDA margin | Earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortisation as a % of revenue — a proxy for operating efficiency |
| Net margin (27.6%) | Profit after all costs, taxes, and interest as a % of revenue |
| ROCE | Return on Capital Employed — how much profit the company generates per rupee invested in the business |
| QRSAM | Quick Reaction Surface to Air Missile — a missile defence system BEL is building for the Indian Army |
| MALE UAV | Medium Altitude Long Endurance Unmanned Aerial Vehicle — India's first large-scale domestic military drone programme |
| LCA Mark-1A | Light Combat Aircraft — India's domestically designed fighter jet, built by HAL |
| NSE/BSE filing | A regulatory disclosure filed with India's National Stock Exchange or Bombay Stock Exchange — has legal standing |
| Split-order mechanism | Both #1 and #2 bidders (L1 and L2) get contracts — reduces winner-takes-all risk |
| FY27 | Financial Year 2027 (April 2026 – March 2027) |
| YoY | Year on year — comparing this quarter's numbers to the same quarter 12 months ago |
| PAT | Profit After Tax |
| EPC scope | Engineering, Procurement, Construction — full project delivery, not just supplying equipment |
