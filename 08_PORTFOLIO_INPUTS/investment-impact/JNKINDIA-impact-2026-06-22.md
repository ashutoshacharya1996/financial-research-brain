## Investment Impact Layer

### Growth Impact
- **Current growth trajectory:** FY26 revenue ₹285 crore, guidance FY27 ₹350–380 crore (26–33% growth)
- **Impact if signal plays out:** Export order in H2 FY27 adds ~8–15% incremental revenue if order is ₹30–50 crore (typical scale); opens export segment representing 10–15% of revenue by FY28
- **Strength of impact:** **Meaningful** — validates capex expansion and diversifies revenue away from OEM concentration
- **Timeline:** Visible in Q3/Q4 FY27 results (6–9 months)

### Margin Impact
- **Current margin regime:** FY26 EBITDA margin ~22%; last 4Q trend stable to improving
- **Impact if signal plays out:** Export orders typically trade at +2–5pts premium to domestic OEM. If ₹40 crore order books at 25% EBITDA margin vs domestic 22%, incremental margin ~120bps at scale
- **Strength of impact:** **Marginal to Meaningful** — bulk of benefit requires repeat orders and volume ramp
- **Timeline:** H2 FY27 + full visibility FY28

### Cash Conversion
- **Working capital profile:** Q4 FY26 DSO 34 days (down from 42 in Q3), inventory/revenue at historic lows, payables extended slightly. Strong position.
- **Impact if signal plays out:** TA'ZIZ order: ADNOC typically requires advance payment or L/C (strong cash terms). Capex to add capacity: ₹40–60 crore estimated (disclosed as part of FY26–27 plan). Company has stated cash available for capex + dividend.
- **Risk:** **Low** — working capital profile healthy, order terms likely strong, company not capital-constrained
- **Recommendation:** No portfolio-level blocker here; green light

### Moat / Competitive Position
- **Is this defensible?** JNKINDIA is established supplier to Indian oil majors (IOCL, BPCL) and global OEMs. TA'ZIZ ADNOC order indicates international buyer confidence; manufacturing capacity + quality certifications are defensible.
- **Is it repeatable?** ADNOC supply chain typically uses multiple qualified suppliers; winning one order improves standing for future orders. Export of this type (Gulf region manufacturing) is repeatable if capacity/quality sustained.
- **Is it at risk?** Competitive risk: other Indian waste-gas vendors (Thermax, Cummins) could bid on similar. Tariff/geopolitical risk if India-UAE trade tensions emerge. Execution risk: capacity ramp delays could hurt repeat orders.
- **Moat assessment:** Medium. Defensible in next 1–2 years; long-term depends on execution.
