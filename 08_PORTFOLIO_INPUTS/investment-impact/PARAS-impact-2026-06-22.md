## Opportunity Signal

**What changed this week:**
PARAS issued its first-ever management revenue guidance — "30-40% revenue growth in FY27 and FY28 while maintaining or growing profitability" — at three separate institutional forums including a London roadshow attended by GIC, Temasek, and global funds. This follows confirmation of the BEL electro-optics subcontract (₹52.82 crore, September 2027 execution). The combination of a new subcontract + first guidance + institutional roadshow represents a qualitative change in management posture that was not present in prior tracked periods.

**Why it matters:**
A small-cap defence company without an earnings call issuing a two-year revenue commitment at a sovereign wealth fund roadshow is high-quality governance signal — management does not make this kind of commitment without internal visibility. The Sight-25HD product platform (electro-optics for FRCV/FICV/RCWS/armoured vehicles) represents an estimated >5,000 unit opportunity over 3–7 years. India defence production at ₹1.78 lakh crore FY26 (record; private sector 24% all-time high) provides the macro environment in which PARAS's guidance is plausible.
