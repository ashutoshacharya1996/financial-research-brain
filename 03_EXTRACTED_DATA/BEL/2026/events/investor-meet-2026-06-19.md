## Guidance

- FY27 order inflow target: >₹55,000 crore (QRSAM expected as largest single contributor)
- QRSAM contract expected to be officially signed by July 2026; initial deliveries within 18 months of signing (est. January 2028)
- Motilal Oswal (June 18, 2026) rates BEL top pick in defence with ~20% upside; bullish on sector catalyst from QRSAM + record defence production data

## Management Commentary — Demand

From post-meet analyst commentary:

- QRSAM: "expected to be officially signed by July 2026, with initial product deliveries commencing within 18 months"
- Additional FY27 pipeline beyond QRSAM: P-75I submarines, Next-Generation Advanced Medium Combat Aircraft (AMCA), data centre solutions
- Long-term FY27 inflow pipeline: QRSAM + P-75I + AMCA + incremental orders
- India's defence production hitting Rs 1.78 lakh crore FY26 record (+15.6% YoY) cited as macro tailwind in investor meet context

## Management Commentary — Margins

- FY27 EBITDA margin guidance: >28% (maintained; no compression expected despite scale-up)
- R&D investment target FY27: ₹2,200 crore (vs ₹1,800 crore area FY26 actuals) — scaling up for AMCA, EW, AI integration

## Management Commentary — Capacity / Expansion

- Capex FY27: >₹1,200 crore (>20% step-up vs FY26) — in part to support QRSAM and AMCA programme readiness
- R&D: ₹2,200 crore FY27 (>6–7% of projected turnover)

## Named Catalysts

- **QRSAM signing (July 2026 expected)**: ~₹30,000 crore total; BEL's share ~₹21,000 crore (~70%); will add ~28% to existing order book of ₹73,882 crore
- **P-75I Submarine Programme**: Naval electronics subsystems; additive to FY27 order flow
- **AMCA (Next-Gen Combat Aircraft)**: Avionics, radar, EW integration — early-stage but large long-cycle programme
- **Data Centre Solutions**: Non-defence diversification; margin profile not stated
- **Macro catalyst**: India defence production FY26 record Rs 1.78 lakh crore — budget and procurement cycle underpins BEL's multi-year order visibility

## Named Risks

- QRSAM already delayed from February/March 2026 to July 2026 — further delay remains a risk
- FY27 order inflow target (>₹55,000 crore) is 83% above FY26 actuals (₹30,000 crore) — ambitious; highly dependent on QRSAM signing
- Delivery within 18 months of QRSAM signing → production ramp risk in FY27/FY28
- BDL (30% of QRSAM contract as missile supplier) introduces supply chain dependency
