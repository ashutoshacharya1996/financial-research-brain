## Guidance

- Target sunroof JV EBITDA margin: 12–14% (range guidance)
- Sunroof JV shareholding: 65% Gabriel / 35% Inalfa (revised after regulatory challenges — previously different split)
- "The decline [in Q4 sunroof revenue vs prior] was due to ramp-up issues in the last quarter"
- SK operations have started; Genos plant construction ongoing — commercial production expected Q4 CY26
- Management long-term target: Group revenue ₹50,000 crore by 2030

## Management Commentary — Demand

- Revenue outperformed industry growth at +19% YoY in Q4 FY26 — characterised as "strong revenue growth amid market challenges" (GuruFocus headline)
- EV two-wheelers: "30% jump in EV two-wheelers" — demand signal in a new segment for Gabriel
- New OEM customers: orders secured from Mahindra and Mobis

## Management Commentary — Margins

- Standalone EBITDA grew 16% vs revenue 19% in Q4 — slight margin dilution; framed as temporary
- Sunroof JV target margin 12–14%; Q4 FY26 actuals at 14.6% — within target; FY26 at 15.1% — above target
- "Margins improved due to operational efficiencies" in sunroof JV
- Phillip Capital note: "sunroofs to drive margin" — incremental margin contribution expected as sunroof revenue scales

## Management Commentary — Capacity / Expansion

- SK operations have started (new suspension plant)
- Genos construction ongoing; commercial production expected Q4 CY26 (October–December 2026)
- Fastener plant: ₹60–70 crore capex planned — new product vertical
- New business areas confirmed in investor presentation: solar dampers JV, lubricants JV
- New customers: Mahindra and Mobis (new OEM additions beyond existing auto component customer base)

## Named Catalysts

- **Genos commercial production (Q4 CY26)**: New plant starting by October–December 2026 — adds revenue capacity
- **Sunroof JV scale-up**: 170,000 units FY26; as volumes grow, margin leverage expected (12–14% target range; already at 15.1% FY26)
- **Solar dampers JV**: Emerging product; no revenue yet but positioned as EV-adjacency play
- **Lubricants JV**: Diversification into automotive lubricants — no revenue yet
- **EV two-wheelers**: +30% growth in this segment; Gabriel's shock absorbers and suspension components for EV 2Ws growing
- **New OEM relationships**: Mahindra (largest Indian auto OEM) and Mobis (Hyundai's Tier-1 subsidiary) — quality signal for Gabriel's product acceptance

- Standalone EBITDA growing slower than revenue in Q4 — margin pressure to monitor
- Group revenue target ₹50,000 crore by 2030 implies ~10x from current ₹4,667 crore consolidated — aspirational target with high execution dependency
- EV two-wheeler exposure: +30% growth positive, but absolute scale not yet disclosed; EV transition timeline uncertain
