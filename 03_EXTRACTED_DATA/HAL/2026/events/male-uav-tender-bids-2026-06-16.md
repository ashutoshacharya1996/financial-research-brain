## Management Commentary — Capacity / Expansion

- Order structure: Split between 2 lowest bidders (L1 and L2) to establish 2 separate production lines — reduces single-point risk for IAF
- Production lines to be set up domestically as part of Aatmanirbhar Bharat mandate

## Named Catalysts

- **IAF MALE UAV Tender Award**: ₹30,000 crore; 87 units; HAL among 10 bidders — award decision pending post June 16
- Strategic context: If HAL wins (or co-wins with L2 split), this becomes the single largest new order programme in HAL's history since the LCA Tejas Mark-1A
- Split order mechanism means two companies will receive production orders — reduces winner-takes-all risk

## Named Risks

- Competitive bid: 10 bidders for 87 units — HAL faces Adani Defence, Tata Advanced Systems, and L&T as direct competitors
- Split order means HAL cannot expect full ₹30,000 crore even if L1
- Bid evaluation timeline unknown — could delay revenue recognition significantly
- UAV is a newer product category for HAL relative to aircraft — execution risk on production at scale
