## Potential Losers

- **Pure ICE component makers** with no EV transition roadmap — exposed to gradual volume decline as OEMs shift platform mix
- **Traditional shock absorber makers** without premium product adjacencies (Gabriel's existing peers who do not diversify)

## Game Theory View

**The transition dynamic:** Auto OEMs are pulling forward EV platform investments (Maruti, Tata, Mahindra all announced EV lines). Component suppliers face a binary choice: build EV-compatible products or lose OEM allocations to new suppliers.

**Who captures:** First movers with existing OEM relationships AND new EV product capability — Gabriel is playing this correctly (using existing Maruti/Tata relationships as distribution for new products like sunroofs).

**Who gets squeezed:** Traditional tier-2 ICE component specialists without product roadmap flexibility.

## Counter Arguments

- Gabriel's ₹50,000 crore group revenue by 2030 target implies ~12x current revenue — extremely aggressive; investor should treat as aspiration, not guidance
- Sunroofs and solar dampers are not pure EV products — they appear on ICE vehicles too; the "EV adjacency" framing may be overstated
- JNK India's green hydrogen signal is very early — no orders confirmed; "mentioned" ≠ "ordered"
- EV penetration in India remains below 5% of total new vehicle sales — domestic EV transition is slower than management narratives suggest
