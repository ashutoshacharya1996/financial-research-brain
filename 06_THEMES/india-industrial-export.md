## Game Theory View

**The prize:** Indian industrial companies breaking into Middle East, Africa, and SE Asia as China+1 manufacturing gives them cost and geography advantages.

**Who captures it:** Companies with proven international execution AND proprietary product technology (JNKINDIA's niche fired heater equipment). Generic EPC contractors face more competition.

**The risk:** Middle East is the primary international market — LT's execution problem highlights that this region has unique supply chain challenges. A concentration risk, not just an opportunity.

## Counter Arguments

- LT's experience: International execution in the Middle East is harder than domestic; supply chain disruptions caused a 300 bps revenue guidance miss for India's best EPC contractor; LT's Q4 FY26 PAT -3% YoY confirms ongoing execution pressure
- JNKINDIA's UAE order: Company's first significant export — unproven international execution capability; December 2027 deadline is 18 months away, many things can go wrong
- 97.5% of JNKINDIA's order book was domestic as of March 31, 2026 — export is early-stage, not a scaled thesis yet
- Single reference customer (ADNOC) does not validate the broader GCC/international market; JNKINDIA needs 2–3 more international orders before the export theme is confirmed
