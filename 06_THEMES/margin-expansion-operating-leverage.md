## Game Theory View

**The pattern:** Indian mid-caps with proprietary products (GABRIEL's suspension tech; PARAS's electro-optics) are moving through a classic S-curve: early-phase investment compresses margins; maturation phase delivers operating leverage as fixed costs are absorbed across higher volumes.

**Who captures most:** Companies with durable pricing power — where the proprietary product, not commodity input costs, sets the margin floor. GABRIEL's OEM relationships and PARAS's captive defence PSU demand both qualify.

**Who gets squeezed out:** Generic component manufacturers without pricing power; as Indian OEMs grow, tier-2 suppliers without differentiated products face continuous margin pressure from procurement consolidation.

## Counter Arguments

- GABRIEL's 55% PAT CAGR over FY26–28 requires very high execution — any new product delay, margin compression, or demand softness breaks the model; 35x FY28 EPS valuation provides no safety margin
- PARAS: PAT leverage (+89% vs +58% revenue) may reflect non-operational items (tax benefit, lower interest) rather than true operating leverage; no earnings call to clarify
- Only 2 companies — below what's needed for a structurally confirmed theme; needs 2–3 more mid-cap industrials showing the same pattern before the theme has standalone conviction
- LT is a counter-case at scale: margin compression at ₹82,762 crore quarterly revenue despite massive order book — operating leverage is not automatic
