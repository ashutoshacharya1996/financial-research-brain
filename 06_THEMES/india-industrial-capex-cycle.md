## Game Theory View

**Who captures the economics:** System integrators and EPC contractors (LT, L&T Technology Services) capture the highest margin layer; equipment manufacturers (BEL, Siemens, ABB) get the next layer; component suppliers (JNKINDIA, Polycab, KEI) compete for the commodity layer.

**Who gets squeezed:** Engineering service companies with no manufacturing moat — they win contracts but face pressure from vertically integrated players.

**Who gains bargaining power:** Specialised component manufacturers with proprietary technology (JNKINDIA's fired heater systems, BEL's electro-optics, Paras's space tech) — cannot easily be substituted.

## Counter Arguments

- L&T FY26 revenue MISSED guidance (12% vs 15%) due to Middle East execution disruptions — strong orders ≠ immediate revenue; execution lag is real
- Capex cycle is interest-rate sensitive; if RBI raises rates in H2 FY27, some discretionary capex may be deferred
- JNKINDIA margin pressure (15.2% EBITDA) despite strong order book — capacity constraints could compress margins further
